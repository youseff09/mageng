RACIAL IDEOLOGY

THE 1920s VS. THE 2020s

SALMA • YUAN • YOUSSEF M • YOUSSEF F • MARYAM [cite: 5]

WHAT IS IT?

Racialized ideology is the belief that race determines value[cite: 36]. In the 1920s, this was a tool used to protect power and privilege[cite: 36]. Today, it has evolved into a system that is hidden but still keeps true success out of reach[cite: 46, 79].

THE BUCHANAN MINDSET

In *The Great Gatsby*, Tom Buchanan represents the racial fear of his era[cite: 37]. He believed white people were losing control, reflecting the real-world Jim Crow laws that enforced segregation to keep wealth in white hands[cite: 37, 39, 42].

SYSTEMIC HURDLES

Racism in the 2020s is structural[cite: 67]. It’s built into how schools are funded and how people are hired[cite: 48]. While progress has been made since the 1920s, inequality is simply more hidden now[cite: 46, 69].

THE AMERICAN DREAM

Fitzgerald concluded that the Dream is deeply unfair[cite: 79]. Though it promises success to anyone who works hard, the system still favors those with existing advantages[cite: 77, 79]. It remains a race with unequal hurdles[cite: 54].

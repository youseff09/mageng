THE 1920s

The decade of Jazz and prohibition was built on a belief that white superiority was the natural order[cite: 35, 38]. While wealth grew, Jim Crow laws enforced segregation, keeping success as a prize for white Americans only[cite: 39, 44].

THE 2020s

Today, racism is no longer "open" like Tom Buchanan's; it is systemic[cite: 46, 47]. It hides in housing codes, school funding, and hiring practices, acting as invisible hurdles in the race for the American Dream[cite: 48, 54].

WHAT CHANGED?

Progress has been made, but the division remains[cite: 69]. The 1920s used laws to exclude; the 2020s uses structures[cite: 66, 67]. Both eras share the same conflict: who truly belongs in America[cite: 68]?

THE VERDICT

Fitzgerald’s novel suggests that the American Dream is structured to keep true success out of reach for most[cite: 79]. Success today is still linked to influence and background rather than pure effort[cite: 73, 77].
